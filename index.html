TOP 10 PLACES TO VISIT IN ZAMBALES

Zambales, located in the Philippines' Central Luzon region, offers enjoyable Zambales activities such as relaxing on sandy beaches, having an intimate camping experience, riding the waves, traveling to a volcano, jumping in an inflatable playground, and more. Zambales features historical sites as well. Ramon Magsaysay, the Philippines' adored President, hails from Castillejos, the province's ancestral home. You can also go to Capones Island, which has a fascinating history and a stunning rocky backdrop.


If you're planning a vacation to Zambales, don't forget to include these tourist attractions and activities in your itinerary.Plan a trip to Zambales, a short beach break from Manila. In this book, you'll find the best sights and must-do activities.Although Zambales is only 3-4 hours away from the bustling metropolis of Manila, it offers a wide range of exciting activities and attractions ideal for families, couples, and groups of friends.


Zambales is one of those areas that has it all: pristine forest landscapes, the Sierra Madre mountain range, living-tradition towns, and 107 miles of pristine beach. There's also a slew of interesting islands and shoals for day trips and peaceful camping.The province comprises 13 municipalities and one city, Olongapo, all of which are serviced by public air-conditioned buses from Metro Manila's Cubao or Pasay. Zambales is located on the west coast of the Philippines, thus surfers can anticipate to have a good time, especially in San Narciso and San Felipe. There are many of resorts in these coastal towns, but The Circle Hostel in San Felipe is the newest addition. It's basic (think three-level bunk beds with no air conditioning), but hey, you can paint on the walls, try slacklining (tightrope walking), and attend monthly yoga classes.


1. Anawangin Cove

"The hidden beach"

It is a famous camping destination in Zambales. Due to the volcanic eruption of Mt. Pinatubo, the sheltered bay has an ash-colored beach.


It is bordered by a large, evergreen forest with towering Agoho trees that look like pine trees. This is what it would look like if Boracay and Baguio had a child.


Anawangin Cove is located in Pundaquit, San Antonio, Zambales. The cove is just 20-30 minutes boat ride from Pundaquit Beach, the jump-off point to the famous coves and island in the town.

2. Hiking and Yangil Tribe Experience in Zambales

" Mesmerizing trails"

In Yangil Village, go on a fantastic hike in Zambales and learn about the Aetas' fascinating culture. Begin your journey at Circle Hostel in Sitio Liwliwa, where you will meet your knowledgeable and kind guide. Spend a day with an indigenous tribe and get up up and personal with them. While walking to the settlement along the volcanic ash trail, take in the spectacular views of the surrounding mountains. You'll plant a tree, try archery, and dance to their music once you arrive! The kind locals will also make a wonderful and filling lunch for you. You'll be astonished to learn that the tribe's livelihood and values are strongly reliant on the environment. You can assist the Aeta tribe rebuild by scheduling this tour.


Hike to the top of the hill for a beautiful view. From there, you should be able to view the turquoise blue ocean surrounded by lush flora.

3. Capones Island

"Crystal clear island"

During the Spanish era, the island was known as "Isla de Gran Capon." On one end, there are rocks, and on the other, there is white sand. It's a popular destination for both surfers and divers.


The island is famous for its lighthouse, which was erected entirely by Filipinos in 1890. The famous landmark is perched atop the rocky outcropping. The tower is ideal for picture shoots since it presents a captivating cinematic scene.

4. Play at Inflatable Island Subic

" The playful resort of zambales"

Prepare to get wet at this floating theme park in Zambales' Subic Bay. Since its debut in 2017, Inflatable Island has become even bigger. It has grown to the size of ten basketball courts placed side by side. It's become a popular tourist destination in Zambales since it's a one-of-a-kind attraction that's just crazy fun.


Inflatable Island is essentially a large playground designed for youngsters and kids-at-heart over the open sea in Subic. You'll never run out of difficulties here, from rickety bridges to the fan favorite Tarzan swing. Is this the biggest draw yet? The enormous unicorn is pure cuteness overload!


When you're tired or need a break, you can relax on the island's pink or yellow beaches.

5.Hike Mount Pinatubo

" The dormant volcano"

Mt. Pinatubo is one of the most popular tourist destinations in Central Luzon, encompassing not just Zambales but also Pampanga and Tarlac. Trekking and 4x4 vehicles can be used to explore this active volcano's rough lahar paths.


The journey and 4x4 ride will be well worth it once you arrive at the crater lake, which has water that varies in hue from blue-green to a deep blue depending on the season.

6. Crystal Beach, San Narciso

" The famous resort"

Crystal Beach Resort offers a beautiful experience that combines nature and luxury. It is easily accessible in San Narciso, Zambales. It's a popular gathering spot for adrenaline seekers, particularly surfers and amateurs who want to ride strong waves near the metro.


From air-conditioned accommodations to glamping tents, the resort has something for everyone. Their apartments should be commended for embracing their indigenous roots, as they have archaic Nipa hut design characteristics.


It's also a pet-friendly hotel! So feel free to bring your four-legged companions on your trip to Zambales

7. Potipot Island

" The little island"

Potipot is without a doubt one of the most accessible islands in the country. A motorized banca or outrigger boat can be rented from Uacon in Candelaria, only a little distance off the main highway, and taken to the island in 5-10 minutes. Potipot has grown in popularity in recent years. We arrived on a Monday, which was good because the site is reported to be crowded on weekends. Fortunately, the island has retained its natural beauty. Potipot itself has no resorts, though there are a few huts for rent if you want to stay the night. There are basic bathrooms and shower rooms (but no showers — bathing requires retrieving water from a deep well).

8.Ride Horses at El Kabayo Stables

" Horses of zambales"

El Kabayo is undoubtedly the best venue in the country to learn and ride horses. The facility is located in Zambales' Subic Bay Freeport Zone and is home to healthy and friendly horses.


With its well-kept gardens, ample ground area, and country-style architecture, the ranch is simply appealing. Wildlife In Need, a non-profit organization specializing in animal care and welfare, oversees the stables.


All revenues from the horseback riding activities go directly to the care and welfare of the lovely horses. Horseback riding lessons and a guided excursion through the lush woodland around El Kabayo Falls are available.


Feel free to escape on horseback in El Salvador, whether you're an equestrian or just want to hang out with horses.

9.Harvest Mangoes at Rosa Farms

" Delicious mangoes of zambales"

The Philippine Carabao Mango is found in Zambales. Rosa Farms has some sweet mangoes that you may pick and eat. It's a 12.5-hectare mango orchard in San Marcelino-San Antonio Boundary, Zambales, located along the National Highway.


The mangoes, the herb garden, the swing, the country store, and the café are just a few of the delights on offer. The farm's "Select & Pay" service allows visitors to pick mangoes directly from the trees. It's ideal for mango-obsessed children and adults!

10.Learn History at Ramon Magsaysay Museum

" The history of the past"

Visit the ancestral home of famous former Philippine President Ramon Magsaysay in Castillejos on a side excursion to learn about Zambales' history. In 1990, the home was refurbished and turned into a museum.


After being severely damaged by the Mt. Pinatubo explosion in 1991, the National Historical Institute rebuilt and refurbished it in 1999.


The modest but lovely two-story edifice houses souvenirs and personal mementos of the previous president, such as photographs and medals.


The historical landmark has some of Ramon Magsaysay's keen Barong Tagalogs on exhibit. The museum also houses his old Presidential automobile, the Willy Jeep.


You should go here to learn more about why Ramon Magsaysay was so well-liked and respected.
